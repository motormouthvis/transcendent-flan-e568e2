Georgetown

Cobblestones, federal-style row houses, and a waterfront promenade.

One of the oldest neighborhoods in the country, Georgetown has been continuously inhabited since long before the city around it was planned. The result is a layered, walkable district where preservation laws keep the original character intact while modern commerce hums along M Street.

Lifestyle

Brunch with the waterfront in view. Boutique shopping that mixes heritage brands with one-off ateliers. University-town energy from the nearby campus. A quieter residential core just one block off the main drag.

Architecture

Federal, Greek Revival, and Victorian, with the occasional modern intrusion that goes through historic-district review before a single shovel touches the soil.
