The Quarter

A walker’s paradise. A music-lover’s home. A foodie’s playground.

What you’ll find

Wrought-iron balconies, brass bands you can hear from your courtyard, shotgun homes painted in colors that have outlived empires. The Quarter is a neighborhood that does not rush.

A day here

Beignets and chicory coffee in the morning. A poke through antique shops at lunch. Jazz pouring out of doorways by five. Po’boys you’ll dream about for a decade.

Architecture

Creole townhouses dominate. Shotguns and camelbacks fill the residential streets. Many homes are protected by preservation ordinances that limit what can be changed on the exterior — which is part of what keeps the character intact.

Why people stay forever

Because few places in America still feel like themselves. The Quarter does. The food, the music, the way light falls on stucco at sunset — if you fall for it, you stay.
