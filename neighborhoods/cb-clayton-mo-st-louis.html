Clayton

A walkable inner-ring suburb known for top schools, a vibrant central business district, and a strong civic identity.

Clayton is the county seat — small in area, large in influence. Tree-lined residential streets give way to a compact downtown of mid-rises, restaurants, and the kind of civic infrastructure (libraries, parks, public art) that makes a place worth coming home to.

Housing

Pre-war single-families dominate the residential streets. Mid-rise condos line the urban edge. Tudors, Colonials, and the occasional architect-designed mid-century all coexist comfortably.

Schools

One of the highest-performing public school districts in the region. Several private and parochial options as well.

Getting around

Two MetroLink stations serve the downtown core. The walking grid is dense and well-shaded. Most commercial blocks have on-street parking.
