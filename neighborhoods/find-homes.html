Old Town

Cobblestone streets, federal-style row houses, and the busiest farmers market on the East Coast.

Why Old Town

It is the kind of neighborhood where you can walk the cobblestones at six in the morning and run into someone you know. The river is at the foot of every cross street. The architecture is preserved by ordinance and beloved by everyone who lives behind it.

Getting around

The metro runs nearby; the water taxi crosses the river; bikes, scooters, and your own two feet handle the rest. Most residents do not own more than one car — and many own none.

What you can buy

Federal-era row houses, mid-century condo conversions, and the rare new-construction infill that goes through extensive historic review.
