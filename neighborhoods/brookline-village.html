Brookline Village

A walkable, transit-rich pocket of one of New England’s most beloved suburbs.

Brookline Village is anchored by its eponymous Green Line stop, ringed by family-owned restaurants, and lined with handsome Victorians and brick triple-deckers. The village pocket is what residents mean when they say “walking distance” here — everything you need is within ten minutes on foot.

Day-to-day

The morning coffee crawl. The Sunday farmers market. The Tuesday-night trivia at the bar that’s been there for thirty years. Brookline Village isn’t flashy — it’s loved.

Housing stock

Victorians, triple-deckers, and a sprinkling of mid-century condo conversions. Inventory is thin and well-priced units don’t last.

Schools

Public schools serving this pocket are among the strongest in the state, with high parent involvement and several long-running magnet programs.
